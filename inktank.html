Overview

As a UX Design student at Career Foundry, I was asked to create a mobile app for tattoo enthusiasts to explore designs and connect with artists. Having a tattoo, I understood how important it is to choose the right tattoo, mine took me 6 years to decide where and what to get. I would’ve loved to have an app that would guide me in finding the perfect tattoo.

Problem

Ink Tank users need a simple way to find a tattoo design. A stress-free experience from being inspired all the way to booking the appointment.

Goal

Provide users with an intuitive way to browse designs, save designs, connect with artists, and find reputable studios near them.

Method

Market research, Interviews, User flow, Wireframes, Mood board, Competitive Analysis, Affinity Mapping, User Journey & Agile Development

Research

Research Goals

Define users’ motivations, needs, and frustrations when looking for tattoo designs and artists. Understand what makes for a great experience for a user when getting a tattoo. So many emotions are involved when getting a tattoo, I want to Identify frustrations and pain point for users when searching for designs Define what behaviors users have while searching for an artist and what is important to them to learn about the artist.

Competitor Research

I conducted competitor research of two apps, looking for strengths and weaknesses. This helped me to see what was out there for users to find tattoo designs, discover artists and locate studios. This was a great opportunity to see what was working in each app that I could possibly use. Also to see what wasn’t working as well and could, in turn, be improved for Ink Tanks users.

See full Competitive Analysis

Surveys and Interviews

I conducted online surveys and 4 in-person interviews, including one tattoo artist. This gave me great insight as to what users need and what they would find most helpful when searching for their next tattoo. I found a lot of inspiration talking with participants about what they chose and why.

Having an artist profile and portfolio is of high priority to most users most tattoos people have, have some sort of personal meaning. The AR feature is a good option but needs some improvement as to how large the pictures are, it would be good to be able to get current tattoos in the picture for someone who is adding on.

Providing a high volume of designs is very important. Finding an artist who specializes in portraits and other specific types of tattoos isn't as easy as one would think. Referrals seem to be the most powerful way to find an artist

Personas

After conducting competitor research and user interviews I created four personas, each having their own specific needs. Creating personas helped to understand and empathize with end-users. These personas allowed me to step out of myself, recognize the different needs and expectations of each user. Each user will have their own experience getting their tattoo, I want their journey finding this tattoo to be enjoyable and memorable.

See Personas

User Journeys

After getting to know my personas I sent them on a journey to explore the app. They expressed what they need, their goals, and the thoughts they may have. This showed me opportunities for the design, the user thought process, and how I can create as little frustrations for them as possible.

See User Journeys

User Flows

I wouldn't want to leave the user hanging in the middle of a design search. I created user flows to walk each persona through a specific task ensuring an effortless transition. In the user flow I took Meghan from finding a design through a set of steps towards a successful outcome and final action, of saving her design to her Ink board.

See User Flows

Site Map and Card Sort

With these personas and tasks in mind, I created a site map. This site map allowed me to have an overall view of organization, hierarchy, and flow. Once the site map was finished I conducted an online card sort using Optimal Sort.

Having repetitive information can be confusing and a waste of time. I simplified categories, moved featured artists, design, and studio under their respective categories. Every participant chose a different location for where to place the request appointment, I will place with my appointments in an effort to make it simple to find.
